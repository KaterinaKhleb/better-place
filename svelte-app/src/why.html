Why is it important to separate waste?

For a few reasons:

  • Toxic polution. Not separated waste will get all mixed eventually, which will result in chemical reaction between the components and will lead to contaminating ground water and realease explosive methane gas.
  • Methane, which is a green house gas, which ultimately leads to climate change, extreme climates and droughts. We can see the impact already in the world.
  • Segregation protects health. When ragpickers put their hands into the waste to clean it up, it results in cuts that further leads to infections, resulting in deterioration of health.
  • Economy of the country.When the waste is not separated properly it leads to less recycling because it is not easy to remove materials for recycling. This means many resources are wasted.
